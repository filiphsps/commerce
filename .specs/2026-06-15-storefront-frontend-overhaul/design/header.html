Surface 08 · header + mega-menu

Header & navigation

The mega-menu panel is portaled to <body> and laid full-viewport-width, while its inner card is clamped to page-width and centered in the viewport — so it never lines up under the trigger or with the header's content edges. The fix re-anchors the card to the SAME max-width container the header uses, then anchors the narrower variants under their trigger. Three CMS-selectable variants (editorial-columns, compact-list, featured-promo) recolor per-shop from the --header-* tokens.

1 The offset bug

Root cause in header-menu.tsx: the portaled positioner is styled position:fixed; top:trigger.bottom; left:0; right:0 (full viewport width), and its inner card is mx-auto max-w-(--page-width) — so the card centers in the viewport, not under the trigger and not against the header's padded content edges. The header bar lives in a centered max-w-(--page-width) container, so the two never agree. Dashed guides below mark the header content edges.

Keep top = trigger.bottom with one consistent gap.

2 Header bar

Sticky bar: logo wordmark left, nav links (Shop, Men, Women, Sale) with chevrons center, search / account / cart with a count bubble right. Below it, the open editorial-columns panel — its card now shares the header's container, so its edges align with the bar's content and the accent rail (before:bg-primary) tops the card.

Top layer & gapThe real panel still portals to <body> with position:fixed (so it escapes the nav's overflow-x-auto clip), but the inner card now sits in the same padded max-w-(--page-width) wrapper as the bar. One consistent gap (top = trigger.bottom + pt-3) bridges the trigger→panel hover traversal.

3 Three variants

Each top-level nav item picks one of the real HEADER_VARIANTS. All three share the same card chrome (tokenized pad / radius / shadow / accent rail) and differ only in interior layout. The narrower two anchor under the trigger; editorial spans the content width.

Per-shop configNav is a recursive CMS tree (packages/cms/src/fields/nav-item.ts): every level carries link, image, description, backgroundColor, items. Each top-level item sets variant to one of the real HEADER_VARIANTS — so a tenant configures Shop as editorial-columns, Sale as compact-list, and a campaign as featured-promo without code changes.
