Surface 02 · recommended-products rail

Product carousel

The recommendations rail composes the SAME .pc card as every other surface, but two layout choices break it. The rail-carousel utility bleeds its vertical scroll-shadow with negative block margins and parks its arrows at left:-8px — any ancestor with overflow:hidden shears both off. And scroll-snap-type:x mandatory paired with a 0.95-threshold IntersectionObserver forces the last card flush into view. The fix re-routes the bleed to inline gutters and moves the fade + arrows onto the wrap.

1 The clipped-edge bug

Root cause in globals.css: the rail uses margin-block:-0.5rem;padding-block:0.5rem to let the card hover-lift shadow bleed past its own box, and the prev/next arrows live at left:-8px/right:-8px. The moment a parent clips overflow, that bleed disappears — the edge cards look shadow-less and the arrows render as half-moons. scroll-snap-type:x mandatory compounds it, slamming the last card flush against the clip edge with no peek.

2 The rail (fixed)

A real "You may also like" block: .sec-head for the title + "View all", then .pc-rail-wrap > .pc-rail rendering the one true .pc card. Fade masks live on .pc-rail-wrap::before/::after (which never clip), toggled by data-at-start/data-at-end; arrows sit INSIDE the wrap at 0.25rem and hide on pointer:coarse. The wrap below carries data-at-start, so the left fade is off and the prev arrow is [hidden].
