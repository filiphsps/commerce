Surface 09 · footer

Footer & iOS safe area

The site footer is sound — but on iOS a thin white strip appears below it, pinned to the very bottom of the screen. The footer's dark background stops short of the home-indicator inset, so the page body background shows through env(safe-area-inset-bottom). The fix is two lines: ship viewport-fit=cover so the inset is non-zero, then pad the footer by env(safe-area-inset-bottom) so the footer's own color fills the inset and reaches the physical edge.

1 The white-gap bug

Both frames are the same page on the same iPhone. The dashed line marks where the footer's background ends and the home-indicator inset begins. Before: the footer paints to its box edge and the body background (white) fills the inset — a white bar under a black footer. After: the footer's background extends through the inset, so the black reaches the bottom; the home indicator just rides on top.

CauseThe footer's dark background only paints to its box edge. The home-indicator area (env(safe-area-inset-bottom)) is filled by whatever is behind it — the <body> background, which is white. Net: a white strip pinned below the black footer.
✓
FixAdd viewport-fit=cover to the viewport meta (otherwise the inset is always 0), then padding-bottom: env(safe-area-inset-bottom) on the footer so its OWN color fills the inset down to the physical edge. No body override needed — the footer reaches the bottom.

2 Footer design

A full-bleed dark footer. Light text, borders and the locale/payment chips all read from the shared .on-dark token flip in ui.css — no hard-coded grays. The padding-bottom: env(safe-area-inset-bottom) from §1 lives on .site-footer (commented in this file's CSS), so on a real iPhone the dark band runs to the bottom edge.
